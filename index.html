Church of Starbucks

"Ecclesia", the Greek word for church, predates Christianity. In ancient Greece it wasn't a religious term, it simply referred to "the community". We're using the mildly humorous phrase "Church of Starbucks" to refer to ours.

► View #ChurchOfStarbucks hashtag (plus) tweets that have be posted, plus relatd stuff... like when folks have posted tweets with both the words "church" and "starbucks".

► Send a #ChurchOfStarbucks hashtag tweet... maybe each time you visit Starbucks, or when you wish you had a Starbucks... you get the idea.

The more those two links are used, the more likely you are to meet other Starbucks regulars in your area that also use Twitter, or folks that might not be in your area, but might have similar interests. The goal is to help you find other folks that understand the usefulness of both Starbucks and Twitter, and encourage you to talk with each other.

•     •     •

The overall goal is to try to educate people and promote a new kind of community (the word "church" means community). One that is not authoritarian. One that is distributed. One that, in some respects nobody controls. So instead of having a central place to sign up, and someone that controls that list. The member list is maintained dynamically. You add yourself to it by simply posting to a hashtag. And when you look at that most recent posts to that hashtag, your see folks that are active. So if you want to be considered active, you might get in the habit of posting to the hashtag each time you get a Starbucks coffee.

You can peruse the list from time to time, and look for new people. Then you can check out their Twitter profile, see if they tweet on stuff that interests you, check out their website or podcast if they have one, and ideally reach out them with a tweet, and possibly form a friendship.
