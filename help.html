When in doubt, check the feed

This community works closely with twitter.com/webulite and our RSS feed. If it looks like an artcle or link is missing from the channel, the first place to look is there.

Add your Twitter account to your Discord profile

To eventually get your blog/podcast added to the feed, the first step is to connect your Twitter account to your Discord profile so that people can link from your Discord profile to your Twitter page. Then, on your Twitter profile, save your blog/podcast URL into the "website" field. This way, folks can click on your nickname in Discord, view your Discord profile, and navigate from your Discord profile to your blog/podcast via your Twitter profile.

Conversations are ephemeral

Our Discord server deletes channel posts after a short period of time (about a day). We believe that chat should be ephemeral like real conversations.

Ok... so your channel conversations will be ephemeral, but on Discord, your Direct Messages are not. To overcome this issue you can use a site called hack.chat for your DM conversations. Instead of holding your one to one conversations with Discord DMs... pass the URL of a temporary hack.chat channel to the person you want to talk with, and have them meet you there.

Use this link to enter the channel, then copy and paste the URL into a DM of the person you want to speak with. You can both meet there and chat.
