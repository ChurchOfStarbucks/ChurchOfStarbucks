Community Help

Community chat is ephemeral

Our Discord server deletes channel posts after a short period of time (currently 48 hours). We believe that chat should be ephemeral like real conversations.

Ephemeral "direct message" conversations

Ok... so your channel conversations will be ephemeral, but on Discord, your Direct Messages are not. To overcome this issue we recommend you use a site called hack.chat for your DM conversations. Instead of holding your one to one conversations with Discord DMs... pass the URL of a temporary hack.chat channel to the person you want to talk with, and have them meet you there.

Use this link to enter the channel, then copy and paste the URL into a DM of the person you want to speak with. They can meet you there, and then when the conversations is over and you leave, the conversations will dissappear.

Add your Twitter account to your Discord profile

To eventually get your blog/podcast added to the webulite community Twitter feed, the first step is to connect your Twitter account to your Discord profile so that people can link from your Discord profile to your Twitter page. Then, on your Twitter profile, add your blog/podcast URL the "website" field. This way, folks can navigate from the Discord community to your blog/podcast via your Twitter profile.
